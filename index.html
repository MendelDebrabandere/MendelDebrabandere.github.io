DreamBOTS (Group Project)

This semester for school, we have a big group project where we have to make a full game in one semester. I ended up working with MyMachine to create a game that fulfills the dream machines of kids. This is a big project with loads of gameplay mechanics. We already finished prototyping and have a good idea of what the game will be like. It will be a third person platformer where the player has to solve puzzles (for example: mazes and build Lego structures) and do some simple movement parkour to progress the level (jumps and timed movement), it's a linear game with checkpoints, this game will of course be very child-friendly.

Our engine of choice is Unreal Engine 5.2, all the gameplay code will be done with C++.

Graduation work

I will do my graduation work this semester. This means that I shall research some game development topic and write a research paper about it.

I chose to work on "Open world optimization in Unreal Engine 5".
I will investigate the systems in place to allow giant scale terrains (10x10 km). What are the trade-offs that need to be made for optimization, how do you guarantee that areas in the far distance look like correct representations of their close up counterparts.
I will test different types of worlds: Landscape, foliage, animations
And systems like Nanite, World partitioning, LOD, etc... (especially nanite and how it works)
I will also add a 'sniper mechanic' where the player is allowed to zoom in and see things close up, the systems will also have to account for this mechanic.
I will measure the systems' efficiency by watching the frame rate and the time it takes to perform one Draw() call, I will also compare the visual quality of the different systems.
